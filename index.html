About

I am a physics student at Stony Brook University interested in nuclear physics, the Electron-Ion Collider, computational physics, and quantitative research.

Academic Focus

My academic work centers on high-energy nuclear physics, QCD, parton structure, gluon saturation, and the physics goals of the Electron-Ion Collider.

Computational Skills

I use C++, ROOT, Python, Unix/Linux, numerical simulations, Monte Carlo event generators, and data analysis workflows for physics research.

Career Goals

I plan to pursue a PhD in nuclear physics and contribute to research at the intersection of theory, computation, and experimental data.

Research & Quantitative Work

My work connects nuclear physics, computational modeling, data analysis, and quantitative problem solving.

Gluon Saturation at the EIC

Studying exclusive vector meson production in electron-nucleus collisions to investigate gluon saturation, geometric scaling, and QCD dynamics at small x.

EIC QCD Gluon Saturation

Centrality & Nuclear PDFs

Using neutron energy in the Zero Degree Calorimeter to study collision geometry while implementing spatially dependent nuclear PDFs to examine how parton distributions change inside nuclei.

ZDC nPDF EPS09s PYTHIA

Quantitative Research

Applying mathematical modeling, programming, statistical analysis, simulation validation, and large-scale data processing to physics research and quantitative problem solving.
